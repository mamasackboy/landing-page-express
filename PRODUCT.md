# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small business owners (dog walkers, pilates studios, accountants, food pop-ups) who need a web presence but can't stomach $3k–$5k agency quotes or three-week timelines. They're browsing at 11pm on their phone, tired of discovery calls and Calendly links. They want to pay and get the thing.

## Product Purpose

Sell a $99 same-day landing page service. One page, custom-designed, hand-coded, deployed, source code transferred. The page itself is the storefront, the portfolio, and the closing argument. If the design doesn't look better than what the visitor could get from Wix, the offer collapses.

## Brand Personality

Warm, confident, steady. Like a premium business card on thick cotton stock: you can tell it cost money, but it doesn't shout. Fast without feeling rushed. The person behind this is clearly good at what they do, and the page proves it before the copy says it.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dark SaaS**: aurora gradients, glass cards, gradient text, orange-on-black. The current design.
- **Template builders**: Wix/Squarespace stock-photo grids, "Schedule a call" energy, cookie-cutter sections.
- **Editorial-magazine aesthetic**: display serif + italic + broadsheet grid + mono labels. Not a magazine.
- **Brutalist-utility**: not ironic, not stripped-down-as-a-statement.

## Design Principles

1. **The page is the portfolio.** Every pixel is a live demo of the service being sold. If it looks templated, the business case dies.
2. **Warmth earns trust.** Light, inviting, human. You're asking a stranger to send $99 to someone they've never met. The design should feel like a handshake, not a dark room.
3. **Substance over spectacle.** Pretty and impressive, but because the typography is confident and the spacing breathes, not because of gradients and blur effects.
4. **Speed is the brand.** The page should feel fast to load, fast to scan, fast to decide. No friction, no filler.
5. **Practice what you preach.** If the service promises "custom design, no templates," the page selling it must be visibly, unmistakably custom.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Reduced motion respected. No hover-only functionality. Touch targets minimum 44px. Color contrast 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components.
